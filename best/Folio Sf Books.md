![The Trees of San Francisco | Image](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content/images/frontcover/FxRN7O5xlqsC?fife=w4000-h6000&source=gbs\_api)

<br>

In "The Trees of San Francisco, " Mike Sullivan combines his love for his adopted city and his passion for trees into a practical guide that highlights 71 of San Francisco's most notable trees. Each tree is beautifully photographed and accompanied by its common and scientific names, along with details about its location within the city and specific care requirements. The book also features 13 informative sidelights on topics ranging from the city's wild parrots to the connection between Australian trees and San Francisco's iconic eucalyptus. The second half of the book directs readers on seven detailed tours, complete with full-page color maps and interesting botanical facts, while a two-page neighborhood map points out 25 prime areas for tree-viewing. This compact 172-page book serves as both a comprehensive reference and an invitation to explore San Francisco's rich arboreal landscape.

<br>

The SS Dirlewanger Brigade: The History of the Black Hunters delves into one of World War II's most notorious units.

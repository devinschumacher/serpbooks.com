*Case Studies From the Frontlines*

<br>

Social Work Case Management: Case Studies from the Frontlines is an essential resource that transforms theoretical knowledge into practical skills for health and human service professionals. This comprehensive work introduces the Task-Centered Case Management Model, grounded in person-in-environment, strengths-based, and ecological principles. Through twenty innovative case studies, the book illustrates the complex realities of modern case management practice, offering readers valuable insights into the evolving field of social work.

![Child Welfare | Image](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content/images/frontcover/W1YFEAAAQBAJ?fife=w4000-h6000&source=gbs\_api)

*Preparing Social Workers for Practice in the Field*

<br>

Children's Welfare: A Social Work Perspective covers the historical, theoretical, and practical aspects of child welfare from a social work viewpoint. The authors present multiple practice perspectives to help students understand how to work with children and families at micro, mezzo, and macro levels. The text addresses major issues like child maltreatment as well as specific concerns such as mandated reporting and evidence-based policy prevention. This 199-page book serves as a comprehensive resource for undergraduate and graduate social work students, preparing them for careers in the child welfare field.

<br>

Social support networks play a vital role in human services, offering informal assistance through extended family, friends, and neighbors.

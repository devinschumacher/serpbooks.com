<br>

"Imperial Vancouver Island" chronicles the British Empire's influence on the region from 1850 to 1950, highlighting the social and cultural impact of Imperial officers and other British expatriates who settled on the island during this period. The book examines the historical relationship between Victoria, the primary British port on the North Pacific Coast, and the newly founded city of Vancouver, exploring how these two coastal cities developed distinct Imperial characteristics before and after British naval forces withdrew from Esquimalt in the 1920s.

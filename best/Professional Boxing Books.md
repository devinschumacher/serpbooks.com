<br>

In 1883, the Olympic Club opened its doors in New Orleans, quickly becoming the epicenter of professional boxing in the United States. This meticulously researched book chronicles the club's most significant bouts between 1890 and 1894, including six world championships and seven national or regional titles. Author S. Derby Gisclair highlights the 1892 Fistic Carnival, which featured three world title fights over consecutive days, culminating in the historic matchup between John L. Sullivan and James J. Corbett for the World Heavyweight Championship. The book explores how the club navigated legal challenges, operating under a revised city ordinance and vague state statute that permitted boxing by chartered athletic clubs. Through its detailed accounts of these groundbreaking matches, this work stands as a comprehensive record of the Olympic Club's pivotal role in shaping the sport of boxing in America.

<br>

## [Prejudice](https://serp.ly/@serpbooks/github/amazon/Prejudice?i=stripbooks)

![Prejudice | Image](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content/images/frontcover/jnwpEAAAQBAJ?fife=w4000-h6000&source=gbs\_api)

*A Study in Non-Ideal Epistemology*

<br>

Prejudice: A Study in Non-Ideal Epistemology examines how prejudiced beliefs can arise through various epistemically justified pathways, challenging the notion that such beliefs necessarily stem from irrationality. Endre Begby argues against linking moral responsibility for prejudiced actions to epistemic blame, proposing instead that victims of prejudice should not bear the burden of proving their attackers knew better. Through detailed case studies at individual, collective, and institutional levels, the book develops a systematic framework for understanding how prejudiced beliefs form and persist, with broader implications forfake news, conspiracy theories, and scientific skepticism.

*Reexamining the Pillars of Visual Arts Curricula*

<br>

"What Art Teaches Us" by Timothy Babulski delves into four core components of visual arts education: the elements of art and design principles, human proportion canons, linear perspective, and RYB color theory. The author analyzes current teaching methods for these fundamental concepts, presenting historical background and proposing cognitivist and holistic alternatives that address students' diverse learning needs. This work provides practical guidance for educators and teacher-candidates aiming to incorporate intellectual depth and experiential learning into their art curriculum.

## [GDR Literature in German Curricula and Textbooks](https://serp.ly/@serpbooks/github/amazon/GDR+Literature+in+German+Curricula+and+Textbooks?i=stripbooks)

![GDR Literature in German Curricula and Textbooks | Image](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content/images/frontcover/IHLXEAAAQBAJ?fife=w4000-h6000&source=gbs\_api)

*Exploring the Legacy of GDR Authors, 1985-2015*

<br>

This carefully curated collection analyzes how German Gymnasium textbooks presented GDR literature from 1985 to 2015, examining works by five significant authors: Bertolt Brecht, Johannes R. Becher, Anna Seghers, Wolf Biermann, and Christa Wolf. Elizabeth Priester Steding's study reveals the intricate relationship between literary content and historical context, demonstrating how ideological influences continue to shape educational materials. The research emphasizes the complexity of GDR literary legacy, moving beyond simplistic categorizations and offering valuable insights into curriculum design choices. 

<br>

- Authors: Elizabeth Priester Steding

- Publisher: Springer Nature

- Published Date: September 15, 2023

- Page Count: 194

- Print Type: BOOK

- Categories: Education

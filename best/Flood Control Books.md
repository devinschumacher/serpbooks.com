![Rivers by Design | Image](https://books.google.com/books/publisher/content/images/frontcover/atINdwG8sv4C?fife=w4000-h6000&source=gbs\_api)

*State Power and the Origins of U.S. Flood Control*

<br>

Rivers by Design chronicles the development of the United States' extensive flood control system, examining how regional interests shaped national policy. Karen M. O'Neill traces the emergence of this mammoth infrastructure project from its roots in 19th-century agricultural and commercial demands along the Mississippi and Sacramento Rivers, through to the pivotal Flood Control Act of 1936 that reshaped water management across the country. The book highlights the enduring impact of these early decisions on our current understanding of flood control and environmental stewardship.
